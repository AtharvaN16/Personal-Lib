# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The owner's sister — a single, personal user cataloging her own physical book collection at home. She uses the app casually and repeatedly: scanning a book right after buying or shelving it, or looking a book up later to remember where it lives in the house. Low-stakes, unhurried, ambient use — not a work tool.

## Product Purpose

A personal fullstack app for cataloging books via barcode scanning. The core job: scan a book's barcode, fetch its details from an open book API, then record which room / bookshelf / shelf index it's stored on. Success looks like never having to wonder "which shelf is that book on?" again — a trustworthy, always-current household inventory that's pleasant enough to use that logging a new book doesn't feel like data entry.

## Brand Personality

Cozy, ASMR, chill, hand-drawn, journal/sketchbook. Think a warm reading nook, not a productivity tool — soft, unhurried, tactile. Voice and interactions should feel like turning pages in a personal notebook, not filling out a form.

## Anti-references

No named anti-references given; default to avoiding the built-in AI-slop tells (cold gray SaaS dashboards, gradient text, cream-on-cream low-contrast text, generic identical card grids, sketchy/doodle SVGs, side-stripe borders, glassmorphism-as-default).

## Design Principles

- Cozy over corporate: every surface should read as a personal journal/shelf, not an admin panel.
- Calm interactions: transitions are soft and unhurried; nothing flashes, jolts, or demands urgency.
- Legible warmth: warm palette and hand-drawn touches never come at the cost of readable contrast.
- Respect the single user: this is a household tool for one person, not a multi-tenant SaaS — no over-engineered chrome, settings, or empty-state onboarding funnels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA as a baseline (contrast, focus states, reduced-motion alternatives). No additional specific accommodations requested; treat this as good default practice rather than a hard compliance requirement given the single-user personal context.
